Transforming Government Schools In India For Millions Of Children Through Education Leadership

India has 200 million young people on the cusp of entering the workforce by 2030. But are they prepared to thrive? Our education system currently faces critical challenges. ASER 2022 report reveals a concerning learning gap, with only 42.8% of grade 5 children able to read at a grade 2 level. One reason is a lack of support for education leaders and an ineffective school environment, which limits children's potential and hinders India's future growth.

Our approach involves driving School Improvements by:

  • Enabling the government to empower education leaders through capacity building, mentoring, peer learning, monitoring, and recognition.
  • Transforming schools to create enriching learning experiences.
  • Inspiring civil society organisations across India to collectively work towards transformation in education.

Long Form

Mantra4Change is a non-profit organisation dedicated to transforming public schools in India for millions of children through education leadership. Our approach involves driving School Improvements by enabling the government to empower education leaders through capacity building, mentoring, peer learning, monitoring, and recognition; transforming schools to create enriching learning experiences; and inspiring civil society organisations across India to collectively work towards transformation in education. We strive to address the critical challenges faced by the country's public education system by enabling education leaders and creating a dynamic community of changemakers. Our mission is clear: to ensure that the 200 million young people poised to enter the workforce by 2030 are equipped with the skills and knowledge necessary to thrive in an ever-evolving world. However, we recognise that this goal cannot be achieved without addressing the important challenge of an ineffective school environment, including teaching and parent engagement. At Mantra4Change, we are committed to enabling education leaders to drive sustainable and continuous school improvements across 150,000 schools by 2025. By working hand-in-hand with stakeholders at all levels, we are determined to unlock the full potential of every child and pave the way for India's future growth.

Problem Statement
  • Scale — India's ~14.7 lakh schools serve ~25 crore children, yet 3+ crore remain out of school or drop out before completing secondary education. (Source: UDISE+, NSSO)
  • Quality — Only 1 in 4 Std 5 students can read a Std 2-level text fluently, revealing deep learning gaps despite near-universal enrollment. (Source: ASER)
  • Urgency — Students in government schools lack grade-appropriate foundational skills, leaving them unprepared for future skilling, employment, and India's competitive workforce. (Source: ASER/NAS)

Mantra's approach to developing better outcomes for children is through a systemic education transformation model. We enable ecosystem, state, and district leadership to support the development of conducive learning environments at the school, classroom and community level, leading to better student outcomes — at every level of the education system.

Problem Statement

Theory of Change
ShikshaLokam restores the agency of education leaders through a continuous-improvement cycle: Sense — listen, observe and assess the local context. Make Sense — analyse data and dialogue with peers to prioritise and design improvements. Learn — build knowledge and skills through courses, mentorship and communities of practice. Improve — plan and lead small, goal-oriented micro-improvements that move student outcomes.
